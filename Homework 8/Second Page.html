The Tale of the Minotaur


Theseus and his companions were sent from Athens as a sacrifice. Forced into the Labyrinth, they faced a maze of dark passages.

Quickly, Tesueus revealed his plan to the terrified Athenians and told them to stay near the entrance while he went in search of

the Minotaur, armed with a sword and a ball of thread. Tying the end of the thread firmly to a doorpost, Theseus cautiously picked

his way through the maze, unravelling the ball as he went. He knew that after killing the beast, he would be able to retrace his steps

and escape the maze by following the thread. Suddenly, the terrible beast was upon him. The head of a bull with the body of a man, the

Minotaur was a terrible looking creature. He charged, snorting, with his horns lowered. A desperate fight ensued, until, summoning all

his strength, Theseus plunged his sword through the Minotaur's neck, severing the head from its body.
